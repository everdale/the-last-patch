# BUG-031 — The Kingdom of Penultima

**Full vignette script — first draft for the red pen.**
One genre (JRPG). One broken rule (the game cannot be completed). One joke (the Final Battle has been in rehearsal for four hundred years).

This is the gate document: if this script doesn't make you laugh the way Monkey Island makes you laugh, we walk away having lost a week instead of a year. Kill-rate notes welcome on every line. Nothing here is precious.

> Target play time: ~15 minutes story path, ~25 with everything.
> Player verbs at this point in the game: WALK, TALK, LOOK, USE, and the WRENCH — always available, always literal.

---

## COLD OPEN

*Black screen. A ticket slides in, typed on a terminal that has seen things.*

```
BUG-031 ······································ CRITICAL
Game cannot be completed.
No hero assigned to Final Battle.
Open: 400 days.
Notes from reporter: please. someone. anyone.
```

*The world map. A patchwork of genre-lands. The Debugger walks toward a border where the grass gets suspiciously tall and the color palette warms by exactly one JRPG. Crossing the border, the screen pinches into a battle-swirl transition. The Debugger arrives on the other side mid-stagger, hand on knees.*

**DEBUGGER:** *(to nobody)* I hate the swirl.

## SCENE 1 — THE ROAD TO PENULTIMA

*Tall grass. A save crystal glows beside the road, humming a four-note arpeggio at itself. In the grass, something is moving toward the Debugger at speed.*

*A SLIME bounces out, rears up to attack — and stops itself mid-air. It settles back down, embarrassed.*

**SLIME:** Sorry. Force of habit. We're saving it for tomorrow.
**DEBUGGER:** Saving what?
**SLIME:** *(with enormous reverence)* Everything.

*The slime bows — a slime bowing is just a slime leaning — and adds, politely, the way you'd say "good morning":*

**SLIME:** Tomorrow's the day.

*It bounces off. Every creature in the grass turns out to be queueing. There is a small sign: `ENCOUNTERS RESUME AFTER THE FINAL BATTLE. THANK YOU FOR YOUR PATIENCE.`*

> PLAYER CAN: examine the save crystal (*"The kingdom autosaves at dusk. The save file is named `the_night_before` and has 146,000 hours on it."*), talk to the slime again (it only says "Tomorrow's the day," warmer each time), proceed to town.

## SCENE 2 — TOWN SQUARE

*Penultima. Banners everywhere: `FINAL BATTLE — TOMORROW!` The banners are clearly very old. Some have been patched with newer fabric that also says TOMORROW. A clocktower looms over the square. It has one hand. Where the 12 should be, a single word: **EVE**. A plaque: "Donated by the Merchants' Guild. Accurate for 400 years."*

*Townsfolk greet each other across the square:*

**WOMAN WITH BREAD:** Tomorrow's the day.
**MAN WITH LADDER:** Tomorrow's the day.

*They say it the way you say "lovely weather." Nobody is being ironic. Nobody has ever been ironic here.*

### Hotspot: The Merchant's stall

*Souvenir stall, deep inventory, geological layers of stock. Shirts: `I SURVIVED THE FINAL BATTLE` — amended underneath in smaller and smaller print: `(DRESS REHEARSAL)` `(ATTENDED)` `(HEARD ABOUT)`.*

**MERCHANT:** Tomorrow's the day! What can I get you? Foam sword? Meteor insurance?
**DEBUGGER:** Has the meteor ever hit anyone?
**MERCHANT:** Never had to pay out once. Our record speaks for itself.

> FOR SALE: foam sword (useless, refundable, the refund line is longer than the sales line), meteor insurance (a scroll that just says `NO`), **popcorn** (one serving).
>
> If the player asks about the popcorn:

**MERCHANT:** Ah, you'll be wanting the Princess's usual. One popcorn, extra salt, no hope.
**DEBUGGER:** No hope?
**MERCHANT:** It's a flavor here.

### Hotspot: The abduction schedule

*A noticeboard. Posted like ferry times:*

```
ROYAL ABDUCTIONS — DAILY
  09:00  (matinee)
  14:30
PLEASE DO NOT FEED THE WYVERN.
THE WYVERN IS DENNIS.
```

### Hotspot: Dennis

*A castle guard sits on the fountain edge, on break, wearing the bottom half of a wyvern costume and the top half of his regular armor. He is eating lunch with the visor up. This is DENNIS.*

**DENNIS:** Tomorrow's the day!
**DEBUGGER:** You're the wyvern.
**DENNIS:** I'm *also* second meteor technician. *(beams)* And I do a faint. In the show. When the hero lands the first blow, a guard faints from sheer awe — that's me. Full armor. Three stages. I've been rehearsing the faint for nine years.
**DEBUGGER:** What are the stages?
**DENNIS:** Disbelief. Acceptance. *(reverent pause)* Clatter.

> DENNIS will demonstrate the faint if asked. It is genuinely excellent. He takes a moment to get back up — the armor — and waves off help: "Part of it. Part of it."

### Hotspot: The castle gates

*Wide open. A sign: `REHEARSALS OPEN TO THE PUBLIC. ATTENDANCE IS A CIVIC HONOR. (MANDATORY.)` The Debugger walks in. Nobody stops them. Tourism is the whole point.*

## SCENE 3 — THE THRONE ROOM

*Vast. Torches. A throne like a fist. And rising from behind it, a wall of black wings unfolds, blotting out the torchlight, and a voice like a continental plate:*

**VEXMOURNE IX:** MORTAL. YOU DARE APPROACH THE—

*(stops. squints.)*

**VEXMOURNE:** Where's your sword?
**DEBUGGER:** I have a wrench. I'm here about the ticket.
**VEXMOURNE:** The hero enters from the *east* door. At *dawn*. With the light behind him. We have blocked this scene for four hundred years. *(the wings sag, one feather at a time)* You're not him.

*He sits down on the steps of his own throne and produces a binder. The binder is labeled **FINAL BATTLE — DO NOT LOSE**, is held together with tape, and is annotated in three generations of handwriting. His grandfather's notes are in blood. His father's are in red ink. His are in gel pen, with the anxious little stars of a student who studied very hard.*

**VEXMOURNE:** Four hundred years of rehearsal. My grandfather built this fight. My father refined it. I have given it my *life*. And two days before my debut, the hero—

*(through his teeth)*

**VEXMOURNE:** —transferred. To a *metroidvania*.

*A long pause. Somewhere above, the sound of someone eating popcorn.*

**VEXMOURNE:** *(without looking up)* Good morning, Princess.
**PRINCESS GRENADINE:** *(from a viewing balcony, flatly)* Tomorrow's the day.

*She does not look up from her popcorn either.*

**VEXMOURNE:** *(to the Debugger, standing, deciding something)* You came about the ticket. Fine. There is one fix. The Final Battle must be performed, and there is no hero. You hold... *(looks at the wrench the way a knight looks at a spork)* ...a tool. You will learn the choreography. I will teach you myself.
**DEBUGGER:** How long does the choreography take to learn?
**VEXMOURNE:** My father took thirty years to master the third act.
**DEBUGGER:** I was thinking more like this afternoon.
**VEXMOURNE:** *(opening the binder with the tenderness of a man opening a reliquary)* Then we start with the door.

> From this moment until the finale, the **WRENCH** is live. At any point, during any rehearsal, the player can simply — bonk. For real. The game never suggests it. There is no prompt. The wrench is just in your hand, the whole time, the way it has been the whole game.

## SCENE 4 — THE REHEARSALS

*Three beats. Each one is a small playable thing. Each can be failed, and failure produces content, not walls — after three failures, the show adapts to you, in fiction, with commentary. (Mercy is diegetic. See design notes.)*

### Beat 1 — The Entrance

*The east door. It sticks. It has not been opened in four hundred days, and it was only ever opened by one man. The Debugger shoulders it open on the third try while Vexmourne watches with his arms folded and his wings folded harder.*

*Then: the walk. The player picks a walk and walks the marked line to center stage.*

> WALK OPTIONS: [Heroic] [Humble] [Normal] — all three are wrong.

**VEXMOURNE:** Again. The hero walks like he owns nothing and deserves everything. You walk like you're billing hourly.
**DEBUGGER:** I am billing hourly.
**VEXMOURNE:** *(closing his eyes)* Dennis. The dawn.

*DENNIS, up in the rigging in his wyvern bottoms, swivels an enormous polished mirror-lamp. Artificial dawn floods the east door.*

**VEXMOURNE:** We rehearse with false dawn. The real one is for the show.

*(Third failure mercy: Vexmourne walks the line himself to demonstrate, perfectly, devastatingly — he is a beautiful hero, it's four hundred years in his body, and for a second nobody in the room says anything. Then he notices himself and stops. "WE WILL SIMPLIFY THE BLOCKING.")*

> 🔧 BONK HERE: he is mid-demonstration, doing the hero's walk, when the wrench lands. He gets bonked *as the hero*. Skip to THE BONK — with the extra sting that you hit him during the only moment he ever lets himself play the other part.

### Beat 2 — The Meteor

*Center stage. Worn into the stone floor: two foot-shaped depressions. Four hundred years of heroes' feet, standing on this exact spot.*

**VEXMOURNE:** Stand in the feet.

*High above, Dennis loads the rehearsal meteor — a boulder on a guided rail, scorched from centuries of service.*

**VEXMOURNE:** The meteor has missed the hero's left shoulder by one handspan in every performance for four centuries. Rehearsed missing is the most difficult missing there is. Your role is simple. You do not move. You do not flinch. You trust the work.

> MINIGAME: the meteor falls. The screen fills with prompts: `[SPACE] DODGE!` `[A] BLOCK!` — flashing, urgent, lying. **The correct input is no input.** Touch anything and the Debugger flinches and ruins the take.
>
> *Fail 1:* the meteor technician resets the rail. Vexmourne, calmly: "Again."
> *Fail 2:* **VEXMOURNE:** FOUR HUNDRED YEARS.
> *Fail 3:* Vexmourne lies down on the floor of the throne room, wings spread, staring at the ceiling. The bard immediately begins playing a piece of soft, devastated music. **DEBUGGER:** Is that — did he write sad music for this exact situation? **VEXMOURNE:** *(from the floor)* That is how often it happens.
> *Mercy:* they lower the meteor past your shoulder on a rope, hand over hand. "WE WILL SIMPLIFY THE BLOCKING."

> 🔧 BONK HERE: you bonk him while standing in the hero's footprints, which means the first real blow in the history of the Final Battle is delivered from his hero's mark. He will mention this in a letter, months later, still working through it.

### Beat 3 — The Exchange

*The monologue. Vexmourne takes his mark on the throne steps and becomes — instantly, completely — the Dreadlord. Wings out. Voice like a landslide. It's the real thing, and it is magnificent, and there is no one to be magnificent at except a contractor holding a wrench.*

**VEXMOURNE:** *(performing)* KNEEL, LITTLE LIGHT. THE SUN ITSELF KNOCKED ONCE AT MY GATES, AND I TAUGHT IT TO WAIT—

*The binder dictates the hero's interjection. It must land on the cue: the third pillar cracks (the pillar is pre-scored; the crack is operated, of course, by Dennis, who has run silently from the rigging to the pillar and is visibly out of breath).*

> THE LINE, per the binder: **"Your reign ends at dawn, Dreadlord. The morning does not negotiate."**
>
> DIALOGUE CHOICE at the cue:
> - "Your reign ends at dawn, Dreadlord. The morning does not negotiate." *(correct)*
> - "Your reign ends at dawn-ish." *(Vexmourne breaks character and grips the bridge of his nose where a nose would be)*
> - "I'm here about the ticket." *(the bard plays a single wrong note. From the balcony, the sound of chewing stops — for the first time. Then resumes.)*

*If the player flubs it, Vexmourne doesn't rage. He comes down off the steps, wounded in a quiet way, and gives notes:*

**VEXMOURNE:** The morning. Does not. NEGOTIATE. It is the best line we have. My grandfather wrote it the night before he died. *(beat)* Say it like you've slept.

> 🔧 BONK HERE: mid-monologue, wings at full extension, voice at full landslide — *bonk.* This is the canonical bonk. See THE BONK.

## SCENE 5 — THE DESK *(optional, both paths)*

*During a break, Vexmourne leaves to fetch the prop sword ("the real one is also a prop. it's complicated."). The player is alone with his desk.*

*The drawer is not locked. It has never needed to be. Inside: four hundred drafts of the same ticket.*

*Draft 1, formal, dated 400 days ago:* "TO THE DEPARTMENT OF REALITY: It has come to my attention that the position of Hero remains unfilled—"

*Draft ~200, bargaining:* "I will accept a hero with no experience. I will train them personally. I will accept a tall child."

*The last one is not a draft. It is a carbon copy of the ticket that was actually sent. Gel pen:* "please. someone. anyone."

*Underneath all of it, a job posting that was never put up:*

```
HERO WANTED.
Must enter from the east.
Everything else is teachable.
```

> The game does not comment. There is no music sting. The player closes the drawer themselves.
> FLAG SET: `knows_who_filed_the_ticket`. This changes single lines in both finales — never announced, just there.

## SCENE 6 — THE TOWER *(optional)*

*The princess's balcony. Opera glasses, a rubric, a mountain of popcorn boxes, and PRINCESS GRENADINE, who has the thousand-yard stare of a woman who has seen the same play fourteen thousand times.*

*If the player brings the popcorn (extra salt, no hope), she gestures to the chair next to her. It's the friendliest thing anyone in this kingdom has done.*

**GRENADINE:** Eleven thousand abductions. I have a punch card.
**DEBUGGER:** Do you ever get to do anything else?
**GRENADINE:** I rate them. *(holds up scorecards, fanned, like a weapon)* I built a rubric. Menace, originality, lift technique. I thought it would make them try new things.
**DEBUGGER:** Did it?
**GRENADINE:** They rehearse *to the rubric* now. I am the only person in history to critic-proof herself out of ever being surprised again.

*She watches the rehearsal below for a moment.*

**GRENADINE:** My highest score is a 6.5. Forty years ago a pigeon got into the throne room during the second act. Nobody had blocked it. Nobody knew what it would do. *(quietly, the way other people say a lover's name)* Nobody knew what *anything* would do.

> She is the only person in Penultima who is bored. Everyone else finds the eve sacred. She finds it interminable. She is also the only one who wears the Royal Seal of Skipping — a small medallion. Royalty may be excused from any performance. She has never once used it. "If I leave, and something finally happens, I would die."

# PATH A — THE PERFORMANCE

*The player learns all three beats and says: let's do the show.*

## SCENE A1 — Opening Night (Morning)

*Real dawn. The throne room is packed — attendance is a civic honor (mandatory). Programs are handed out. The program includes the audience's lines:*

```
BEAT 14: GASP (ALL)
BEAT 22: MURMUR, RISING
FINALE: APPLAUSE (STANDING)
INTERMISSION: NONE. THE BATTLE RUNS NINE MINUTES.
```

*The player performs the three beats, under pressure, with the crowd gasping precisely on cue. The east door opens on the first pull — Dennis oiled it at 4 a.m., he tells you later, voice breaking, "it was an honor." The meteor misses by a handspan. The morning does not negotiate.*

*And Vexmourne is — transcendent. Four hundred years arrive at once. For nine minutes he gets to be exactly what three generations built him to be, and the audience gasps where the paper says GASP, and Dennis's faint (Disbelief, Acceptance, Clatter) gets its own round of applause.*

*The hero lands the final blow — choreographed, missing by a handspan, like everything here. Vexmourne falls the way mountains would like to. Hold on his face on the floor: eyes open, looking at the ceiling. The audience cannot see his face from there. Only the player can.*

## SCENE A2 — The Rating

*The hall waits. The Princess stands. Everyone turns. She holds up a card:*

**6.5**

*Gasps — unscripted ones, the first in forty years.*

**GRENADINE:** *(to the hall, deadpan)* The delivery of the hero's line was the worst I have witnessed in fourteen thousand performances. Genuinely, athletically terrible. I had no idea what he would say next. *(sits back down)* Ties the pigeon.

## SCENE A3 — The Morning After

*The square, next day. Silence. The clocktower's one hand points at EVE, which is now wrong, and everyone walks a little too carefully, the way you do in a house where something has died. The merchant stands at his stall, surrounded by ten thousand shirts that say TOMORROW.*

**MERCHANT:** *(to himself, testing it)* ...Yesterday was the day?
*(it doesn't work. He says nothing else. There is nothing else here to say.)*

*By afternoon the kingdom finds its answer, and the answer is the most Penultima answer possible: they will do it again. Daily. The Final Battle becomes the Final Battle (Daily, Dawn, Free For Citizens). Dennis is cast as the permanent hero. He weeps directly onto his contract. The ink runs. They frame it anyway.*

*The Debugger's last visit to the throne room: Vexmourne on the steps, binder open, adding notes in a fourth handwriting — his own gel pen, but the entries are about YOU. Your blocking. Your flubbed line. The understudy notes of a man preparing to do this forever.*

**VEXMOURNE:** *(not unkindly, not looking up)* Same time tomorrow.

```

Resolution: WORKING AS INTENDED.
Note: hero role filled internally. Costume budget doubled.
Final Battle now recurs daily. Status: BY DESIGN.
```

# PATH B — THE BONK

*At any point, during any rehearsal: bonk. For real.*

## SCENE B1 — The Bonk

*The music doesn't stop; the **bard** stops, physically, mid-strum, frozen like a paused video. Dennis faints — no stages, no choreography, just straight down, loudly, in full armor, the worst faint of his life. And Vexmourne touches his cheek like it's the first thing that's ever happened to him.*

**VEXMOURNE:** *(very small)* You hit me. Nobody has ever—

*(eyes shining, turning to the portrait wall — grandfather in oils, father in oils, his own portrait still a charcoal sketch because they weren't sure he'd earned oils yet)*

**VEXMOURNE:** DAD. DAD, DID YOU SEE—

*(catches himself. clears throat. voice like a landslide.)*

**VEXMOURNE:** I MEAN. INSOLENCE.

*If `knows_who_filed_the_ticket`: he looks at the wrench, then at the player, and says, almost a whisper: "You read the drawer." It is not a question, and he does not seem angry.*

**VEXMOURNE:** *(wings rising, but differently now — not blocking, not blotting out the torchlight on schedule — rising the way weather does)* No choreography. No binder. No marks.

*(he kicks the binder across the floor. Four hundred years of tape and gel pen slide under the throne.)*

**VEXMOURNE:** Dreadlord Vexmourne the Ninth would like to fight you. *(beat)* Please.

## SCENE B2 — The Real Fight

*The first real fight in the history of the kingdom. It is also the first nine minutes of pure joy in Vexmourne's life, and he is crying, and he is trying to kill you, and both of these are sincere.*

**Phase 1 — The marks.** *His body knows only the choreography. Every attack he throws is a rehearsal beat played at full speed and full sincerity — which means every attack is built, in his bones, to miss the hero's marks by one handspan. The floor's worn footprints are safety. The fight is a puzzle: cross the stage mark to mark while he tears the blocking apart trying to unlearn himself.*

**VEXMOURNE:** GET OFF YOUR MARK.
**DEBUGGER:** It's a good mark!
**VEXMOURNE:** *(delighted, furious, mid-leap)* FOUR HUNDRED YEARS OF STAGECRAFT AND YOU USE IT TO *CHEAT*—

*He attempts to improvise a battle cry. He has never improvised anything.*

**VEXMOURNE:** YOUR... FACE. IS BAD.
*(beat. he lands. genuine anguish:)*
**VEXMOURNE:** That was my first one. Pretend you didn't hear it.

**Phase 2 — The meteor, inverted.** *He orders the meteor — the REAL call, the one from the sealed fifth act. Dennis (recovered, devastated to be working during history) loads it. The familiar prompt appears: `DO NOT PRESS ANYTHING` — then crosses itself out:*

```
~~DO NOT PRESS ANYTHING~~
PRESS SOMETHING. ANYTHING. NOW.
```

*The meteor is aimed AT the footprints. The safest spot in four hundred years is now the only deadly one. (The game teaching itself in one image: the rules just became real, everywhere.)*

**Phase 3 — The entrance.** *Mid-fight, Vexmourne suddenly disengages, strides to the east door, exits, and re-enters — through the east door, false dawn behind him (Dennis, sobbing, operating the mirror-lamp with one hand and his halberd with the other) — doing the hero's walk. He has waited four hundred years to try the other part. He plays both roles of the Final Battle at once, swapping mid-stage, and it is the stupidest, most beautiful thing the kingdom has ever seen.*

*From the balcony: the sound of chewing stops.*

## SCENE B3 — The Catch

*The last meteor call goes wrong. The real rig was never tested — why would it be — and the rail shears. The meteor leaves its line and falls, not at the player: at Dennis, frozen at the pillar, who has rehearsed a faint for nine years and now, facing the real thing, the actual once-in-four-centuries thing, forgets to do it. He just stands there. Blinking.*

*Vexmourne moves without thinking. Four hundred years of rehearsal, three generations, fourteen thousand performances of controlled, precise, loving failure — and he catches the meteor's path and turns it, exactly, by one handspan past Dennis's left shoulder.*

*Stone dust. Silence. Vexmourne looks at his own hands.*

**VEXMOURNE:** I missed. *(beat)* On purpose. For real.

*And then — from the gallery — the music changes. The bard has broken the seal on the last page of the score: the fifth movement of the Final Battle theme, written four hundred years ago, never once performed, because the fight never finished for real. He plays the ending. The whole kingdom hears, for the first time, that their song had one.*

*The Princess stands. The popcorn falls. She holds up — nothing. Both hands empty.*

**GRENADINE:** *(to herself)* It's not on the rubric.

*She applauds. One person, in a stunned hall, clapping like it's a fight she's winning.*

## SCENE B4 — The Bill

*The kingdom does not throw a parade.*

*The Final Battle is over, which means the Eve is over, which means the tour groups, the abduction schedule, the meteor insurance, the foam swords, the 9:00 matinee, the entire economy and culture and religion of Penultima just closed in one afternoon — because some contractor hit the Dreadlord with a wrench.*

*Walking back through the square: conversations stop. The innkeeper turns the WELCOME sign around as the player passes. (We will see this gesture again, much later, in a church basement with folding chairs. It should be the same animation. — see PATCHED ANONYMOUS, design doc.)*

*Only the merchant waves the player over, urgently, conspiratorially. His stall is empty. Sold out. He flips up one last shirt: `I SURVIVED THE FINAL BATTLE` — no parentheses.*

**MERCHANT:** *(whispering)* True now. Moved the whole stock in an hour. *(presses the shirt into the player's hands, glancing around at the glaring town)* Don't tell anyone I said thank you.

```
BUG-031 ········································ CLOSED
Resolution: FIXED. Hero no longer required.
            The fight became real.

BUG-047 ········································ OPENED
Dreadlord has discovered sincerity.

```

# DESIGN APPENDIX

## The 1+4 law, applied

- **Story bar:** close BUG-031 by either path. Generous. ~15 minutes. Every minigame has diegetic mercy (the show simplifies its own blocking, marks glow brighter after deaths, Dennis eventually holds up cue cards from the wings).
- **Mastery tiers (optional, never gating):**
  1. **Understudy of the Year** — Path A with all three beats clean on the first take.
  2. **Archivist** — find all three desk drafts, the hero's note, and the watering can.
  3. **QA in Production** — bonk within ten seconds of meeting Vexmourne. (The fight is harder: you never learned where the marks are. The achievement is the apology.)
  4. **Dramaturg** — learn all three beats AND read every binder page, *then* bonk. The fight gains extra dialogue: he knows that you knew exactly what you were destroying, and what it was worth.

## Why both paths share the same content

The three rehearsal beats are Path A's performance AND Path B's combat tutorial. Learn the choreography to perform it — or learn it to exploit it (his attacks miss the hero's marks by muscle memory). Every minute of rehearsal pays off in both endings. No content is path-exclusive except the endings themselves.

## The one-absurdity rule, audited

The single absurdity: *the Final Battle has been in rehearsal for four hundred years.* Everything else is that absurdity taken seriously by everyone — the economy (tourism, merch, insurance), the religion (the Eve), the infrastructure (abduction ferry times, the clock), the people (Dennis's three-stage faint, the princess's rubric, the bard's sealed fifth movement). No second absurdity is introduced. The Debugger is the only person who finds any of it strange, and is wrong to.

## Lines most likely to be cut in punch-up
