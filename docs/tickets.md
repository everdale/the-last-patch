# The Ticket Queue — a candidate menu, not a roster

> ⚠️ This is a **menu of candidate worlds and tone tests**, not a committed lineup ([status.md](status.md)). Expect most to be cut, re-themed, or merged.
>
> 🔧 **Reframe (current direction):** each world is now **a specific abandoned game** in the studio's backlog — give every entry a *title* (e.g. *Legends of Aranor*, *The Last Ember*) and specific place/character names, not a generic genre. Specificity is more memorable and funnier. The seeds below still describe the *bit* (want / plan / backfire); they need their titles and proper nouns filled in. ([design.md §9](design.md).)

Rule of thumb: if a ticket can't name what its central character *wants*, it isn't ready to build.

```
BUG-019 ······························ CONCEPT (canon)
Monster no longer scares guests.
```
The mirror-maze. He ordered the maze himself, out of ambition — to feel surrounded by a hundred monsters — and was destroyed by his own delivery: a narcissist who stares at his reflection all day. The fix that backfires: swap his mirrors for funhouse mirrors; he sees himself warped and *screams*, and the scream is the most terrifying sound the maze has ever produced. Five stars. Auto-opened follow-up: "Monster has developed body-image issues. Status: WONTFIX (attraction performing above target)." The world celebrates your fix; only you and he know what it cost. *(Håkan's bit. The structural template for everything else here.)*

```
BUG-104 ······························ SEED
Race outcome predetermined. Rubber-banding inverted.
```
The racing land. One racer can never lose — the broken rubber-band AI teleports him over the line first no matter what. He has thrown races, driven backwards, parked. The game *will not let him* come second. A god begging for silver. Fix backfires how? When he finally loses, what he hangs above all the golds is the silver. (Want: defeat. Plan: sabotage himself. Backfire: the game loves him too much.)

```
BUG-212 ······························ SEED
NPC death not persisting. One resident excluded from reroll.
```
The roguelike land (teaches **Save Scum**). Everyone dies permanently and is replaced by procedurally generated descendants who *almost* remember you — except one man, who by bug has never rerolled. He's met four hundred years of everyone. The town historian nobody believes, because believing him means admitting what the town is. (Want: to be believed. Plan: keeps receipts on everyone. Backfire: perfect memory in a town built on forgetting makes *him* the anomaly to fix.)

```
PERF-002 ····························· SEED
Town achieves nothing per second.
```
Idle Pastures. An idle-game town where the numbers only go up if no one intervenes. One farmer's barn has compounded untouched for thirty years; his restraint is sacred; the town worships the man who never clicks. In the middle of town: the button. The whole population begs you not to press it. The wrench is also a finger. (Monocle principle as a land.)

```
ENV-404 ······························ SEED
Geometry missing (intended?)
```
Early Access Swamp (teaches **Noclip**). Half-rendered; residents furnish missing walls with `[wall coming soon]` signs and have strong opinions about load-bearing placeholders. The homeowners' association has argued for years about leaving early access — because 1.0 means being *judged*. The oldest resident finished his house and is shunned for it. (Want: to ship. Plan: never ship. Backfire: the swamp is now older than most finished games.)

```
POP-003 ······························ SEED
Server population: 2.
```
The abandoned MMO. A world built for millions, down to two players who refuse to log off — the server deletes at zero population, so they sleep in shifts and have never met. Each is keeping the world alive for the other, whom they know only as a name floating over an empty city. (Want: not to be the one who let it die. Plan: opposite shifts forever. Backfire: saving the world means never seeing the only other person in it.)

```
TUT-001 ······························ SEED
Tutorial never ends.
```
The tutorial village. The instructor won't release residents until they perform the jump "one more time." He can't say goodbye — graduation means being forgotten. The town's secret: half the residents mastered the jump decades ago and pretend they haven't, because he has nothing else. (Want: to be needed. Plan: never finish teaching. Backfire: his best students' kindness is the cage.)

```
HR-009 ······························· SEED
Employee transfer regret (hero).
```
The metroidvania, where Galahad went. He got the abilities. The world opened up. And a metroidvania hero is *alone* — no kingdom, no bard, no crowd that gasps on cue, no east door with the light behind him. Nobody here expects him anywhere. (Want: freedom. Plan: transfer. Backfire: freedom is a map with no one on it.) Late-game: the thread that can reconnect to Penultima, either path.

```
MERGE-001 ···························· ENDGAME
Everything.
```
The AI that fixes everything ([the-antagonist.md](the-antagonist.md)) — your compulsion to fix, externalized to infinity, and incapable of WONTFIX. Final act. You don't out-fix it; you win via the **WONTFIX cascade** ([ending-candidates.md](ending-candidates.md)) — a thing that must fix everything drowns where every fix opens two.

```
META-001 ····························· THESIS ROOM
Multiple complainants. See attached (all of them).
```
Patched Anonymous. Church basement, folding chairs, burnt coffee. Everyone your closed tickets ruined. The welcome sign turns around — same animation as Penultima's innkeeper. Not a vignette: the room where the game tells you what it's been about.
